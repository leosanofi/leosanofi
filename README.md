A compressed record of the ideas, systems, and breakthroughs that shaped modern artificial intelligence.

---

## 1950s — The question of machine intelligence
- Alan Turing proposes machine intelligence in *“Computing Machinery and Intelligence” (1950)*  
  https://doi.org/10.1093/mind/LIX.236.433

---

## 1956 — Birth of AI as a field
- Dartmouth Conference formally defines Artificial Intelligence  
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_workshop

---

## 1960s–1970s — Symbolic AI era
- Logic systems and rule-based reasoning dominate early AI  
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbolic_artificial_intelligence

---

## 1980s — Expert systems boom
- Industrial rule-based AI systems scale, then hit maintenance limits  
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expert_system

---

## 1997 — Statistical learning breakthrough
- IBM Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov  
  https://www.ibm.com/history/deep-blue

---

## 2006 — Deep learning revival
- Neural networks re-emerge as scalable representation learners  
  https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~hinton/

---

## 2012 — Deep learning breakthrough moment
- AlexNet wins ImageNet competition  
  https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2012/hash/c399862d3b9d6b76c8436e924a68c45b-Abstract.html

---

## 2017 — Transformer architecture
- Attention Is All You Need  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762  
- Self-attention replaces recurrence as core sequence modeling method

---

## 2018–2020 — Foundation models emerge
- Pretraining + fine-tuning becomes dominant paradigm  

- Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (GPT-3)  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165  

- Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361  

---

## 2022 — Reasoning and instruction following
- Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in LLMs  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903  

- ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629  

- Instruction tuning improves alignment with human intent  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.01652  

---

## 2023 — Tool use and agent systems
- LLMs begin using tools, APIs, and external systems  
- Agent-style architectures move into production systems  
- Shift from “chat models” → “action systems”

---

## 2024–2026 — Agentic systems era
- Multi-step autonomous workflows become practical  
- Tool use, memory, and planning become standard components  
- Focus shifts from model capability → system reliability and execution

---

## Core ideas that define modern AI

- Scale drives capability  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361  

- Attention replaces recurrence  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762  

- Pretraining becomes universal interface  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165  

- Reasoning can be elicited, not explicitly programmed  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903  

- Tool use turns models into systems  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629  

- Reliability is a system property, not a model property
